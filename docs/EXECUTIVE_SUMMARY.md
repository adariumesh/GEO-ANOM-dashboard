## Geospatial Optimization Analysis

**Date:** March 9, 2026

## 🎯 Executive Overview

This report presents a comprehensive geospatial optimization analysis for establishing a network of regional anaerobic digesters to process poultry waste from Maryland's Eastern Shore. Using real data from 442 Animal Feeding Operations (AFOs) representing 56.4 million animals, we analyzed multiple scenarios to determine the optimal configuration.

**Key Recommendation: Implement a 10-hub regional digester network.**

---

## 💼 Business Case

### Investment Required
- **Upfront Capital:** $20 million (10 hubs @ $2M each)
- **Annual Operating Costs:** $6.0 million (primarily transport)

### Financial Returns
- **Annual Revenue:** $38.4 million
  - Energy sales: $19.1M/year (159,087 MWh)
  - Digestate fertilizer: $19.3M/year (1.3M tons)
- **Annual Net Cash Flow:** $32.3 million
- **5-Year NPV:** **$141.7 million**
- **Simple Payback Period:** **7.2 months**
- **5-Year ROI:** 709%

### Environmental Impact
- **Manure Processed:** 1.35 million tons/year
- **Renewable Energy:** 159 GWh/year (powers ~15,000 homes)
- **Nutrient Recovery:** 1.3M tons digestate (reduces Chesapeake Bay pollution)
- **GHG Reduction:** ~50,000 tons CO₂-equivalent/year

### Why 10 Hubs is Optimal

**Economic Efficiency:**
- Highest 5-year NPV ($141.7M)
- Lowest cost per animal ($0.15/year transport)
- Best balance of construction and operating costs

**Operational Feasibility:**
- Serves all 334 reachable AFOs (100% coverage)
- Hub capacity 52-100% (balanced utilization)
- Average transport distance: 28.2 km (reasonable for daily hauls)
- Geographic distribution across 6 counties

**Financial Strength:**
- Revenue 6.4x operating costs
- Positive NPV even in low-revenue scenarios
- 7-month payback provides rapid capital recovery
- Diversified revenue streams (energy + fertilizer)

## 🎯 Strategic Recommendations

### Phase 1: Pilot Deployment (Year 1)
**Recommendation:** Start with 2-3 hubs in Worcester County

**Rationale:**
- Highest AFO density (79 facilities, 12.4M animals)
- 22% of state's animal population in one county
- Strong potential for early partnerships with large facilities
- Manageable scale for proof-of-concept ($4-6M investment)
- Revenue from 3 hubs: ~$11.5M/year vs $1.8M operating costs

**Pilot Objectives:**
- Validate biogas yield assumptions
- Establish transport logistics
- Develop utility offtake agreements
- Create digestate market channels
- Demonstrate financial model

### Phase 2: Regional Expansion (Years 2-3)
- Add 4 hubs in Caroline and Wicomico counties
- Leverage pilot learnings for construction efficiency
- Expand utility contracts
- Scale digestate distribution network

### Phase 3: Full Network (Years 3-5)
- Complete 10-hub network
- Optimize operations across all facilities
- Achieve full 334-AFO coverage
- Realize maximum revenue potential ($38.4M/year)

## ⚠️ Key Assumptions & Limitations

### Assumptions
- $2M construction cost per hub (industry average for 5M animal capacity)
- $2.50/km transport cost (fuel + driver)
- 5M animal capacity per hub (large industrial digester standard)
- $0.12/kWh energy price (Maryland grid average)
- $15/ton digestate price (conservative for organic fertilizer)
- 5-year planning horizon
- Biogas yields based on ASABE standards (0.4 m³/kg VS)
- 35% CHP electrical efficiency (industry standard)

### Limitations
- Analysis does not include:
  - Federal/state renewable energy incentives (would improve economics)
  - Detailed operations costs beyond transport (estimated $2-3M/year)
  - Potential carbon credit revenue
  - Land acquisition costs (assumes siting at or near existing AFOs)
  - Detailed permitting timeline/costs
- 92 AFOs (21%) lack precise coordinates (excluded from optimization)
- Revenue model based on industry averages (pilot will validate)

**Despite these limitations, the financial case is strong with significant safety margins.**

## 🎓 Methodology

This analysis utilized advanced geospatial optimization techniques:

1. **Data Foundation:** 442 AFO permits from Maryland Department of Environment
2. **Optimization Model:** P-Median location-allocation with capacity constraints
3. **Solver:** Integer Linear Programming (PuLP with CBC)
4. **Geospatial Analysis:** GeoPandas, EPSG:2248 projection
5. **Scenario Testing:** 8, 10, 12, 15 hub configurations
6. **Economic Modeling:** NPV, payback, ROI with sensitivity analysis
7. **Revenue Estimation:** ASABE biogas standards, industry CHP performance

**All code, data, and analysis are reproducible and documented.**
